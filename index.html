The Digital Scam Pipeline

How Australians are targeted, tricked and financially harmed through digital scams.

Main dataset Scamwatch 2025 Reports, losses, scam type, contact method, age group and state or territory.
Story pathway Contact → Scam → Loss → Harm The page follows how scam activity moves from first contact to real-world impact.
Audience Everyday Australians Clear, low-jargon explanations for a general audience, not a specialist fraud analyst.

Opening

Digital scams are not just a technology issue.

Scams begin with contact, but the harm does not end at the message, phone call or website. This visualisation shows how scams reach Australians, which scam types create the greatest financial damage, and how the effects continue after the scam is encountered.

What happens after people are targeted?

Scam harm is not only financial. Cybercrime can lead to help-seeking, stress, practical disruption and other consequences that continue after the scam interaction ends.

Final takeaway

Scam harm is uneven.

Digital scams reach Australians through many channels, but the financial and personal impact depends on the scam type, the victim group, location, and what happens after the scam is encountered.
